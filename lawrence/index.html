Droppings and Other Evidence

Pests leave behind various forms of evidence beyond just visible sightings. Droppings are one of the most common and telling signs of an infestation. The appearance and location of droppings can help identify the type of pest. For example, rodent droppings are usually small, dark, and pellet-shaped, often found near food sources or nesting areas. Cockroach droppings can resemble black pepper or coffee grounds and are found in areas they frequent, such as cabinets or behind appliances. Other types of evidence include shed skins, particularly from insects like bed bugs or cockroaches, which they molt as they grow. Gnaw marks or chew marks on wood, cardboard, or plastic are often indicative of rodents. Spider webs can also be a sign, especially if they are numerous or located in high-traffic areas. Finding nesting materials, such as shredded paper, fabric, or insulation tucked away in hidden areas, is another strong indicator of a pest problem, particularly with rodents or birds seeking shelter. Our experienced technicians are skilled at identifying these less obvious signs of pest activity. We know where to look and what to look for, ensuring we accurately assess the extent of the infestation and the types of pests involved. If you discover any of these forms of evidence, it's time to call for professional help. Noises in Walls or Attic

Hearing unusual noises emanating from within your walls, attic, or crawl spaces can be unsettling and is often a strong indicator of a hidden pest problem. These noises can vary depending on the type of pest present. Scratching or scurrying sounds are commonly associated with rodents like mice and rats as they move through wall voids, ceilings, or floors. These noises are often more noticeable at night when these animals are most active. Chewing or gnawing sounds can also point to rodents or even termites working within wooden structures. The buzzing or rustling sounds might indicate the presence of insects, particularly in attics or eaves where bees or wasps might be nesting. In some cases, distinct vocalizations might be heard, especially with larger pests like squirrels or raccoons that have taken up residence in your attic or chimney. Ignoring these noises is not recommended, as it could mean that pests are causing damage or posing health risks within your property's structures.
